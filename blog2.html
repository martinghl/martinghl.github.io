A Door Cracks Open

January 12, 2025

The PhD journey begins not with certainty but with a quiet hope—a whispered dream that weaves its way through long nights and endless drafts. I’ve just completed my applications, each one carrying a piece of my story, a fragment of my ambition, out into the world.

The first responses have arrived, a mix of rejection and possibility. Two doors closed, but one has cracked open—a first interview. I find myself standing at the threshold, heart steady, mind alive with questions. What lies beyond this moment? What could this singular opportunity become?

It’s easy to measure the journey in numbers—applications submitted, schools replied, interviews granted. But those metrics don’t capture the depth of this process. They don’t speak to the resilience it takes to keep believing when rejection knocks or the quiet pride of knowing that even one "yes" can outweigh a hundred "no's."

This is more than a test of worthiness; it’s a chance to align my passion with purpose, to transform uncertainty into growth.

The odds don’t feel so bad, not when I think of the vastness of possibility that a single "yes" can hold. For now, I prepare, not for perfection, but for authenticity. Because isn’t that the essence of every great journey? To step forward, not knowing where the road will lead, but trusting that it’s worth walking.
